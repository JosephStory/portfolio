006 : Programming Experience

My degree in Mechanical Engineering kickstarted my interest in programming, with the course covering both Python and C++. As part of the program, we used Python to develop a flood warning system that utilised live river data. On top of this, we used C++ to create a physics engine for a Mars Rover landing simulation.

Whilst working at the Tun Abdul Razak Research Centre, I used an Arduino Uno and an electronic circuit designed in-house to create an ABS system for a tyre testing trailer. The Arduino Uno analysed the inputs from Ford wheel speed sensors, and used this information to control solenoid valves in a reverse engineered Bosch ABS module.

For my summer research project at the University of Cambridge, I used C++ to develop an algorithm (based on the simulated annealing and greedy techniques) that determined the optimum trajectory for delivery drones. This algorithm provided the optimum route for distance, time, and energy consumption.

In addition to the above work experience, I have also completed a multitude of relevant courses on Udemy to satisfy my passion for technological innovation. These courses include:

  • Complete Python Developer: Zero to Mastery
  • Complete Machine Learning & Data Science Bootcamp
  • Complete SQL & Databases Bootcamp: Zero to Mastery
